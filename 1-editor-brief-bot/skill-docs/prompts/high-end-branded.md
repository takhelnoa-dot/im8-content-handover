# High-End Branded Mode

Use these rules in addition to `shared-rules.md` when `briefType = branded`.

## Cut style

- **Pacing:** measured, allows lines to land. Single-narrative or dual-narrative — no rapid cycling between speakers.
- **Length skew:** lean toward 40-60s base cuts. Solo/kitchen formats can be ~30s. Save 90s for genuinely earned long-form (multi-expert, layered narrative).
- **Narrative shape:** open with founder/credibility frame → build with substance → close with community/impact. The product is the answer, not the climax.
- **Tone:** confident, restrained, premium. Education first. The brand voice is "the standard," not "the deal."

## B-roll vocabulary (branded)

Only use directives that reference clean, polished, cinematic visuals. Acceptable categories:

- Talent in their craft environment (athlete on court, doctor in clinic, DB in gym/kitchen)
- Product preparation shots — natural lighting, premium kitchen settings, hands mixing the powder, shake colour/texture
- Lab / clinical / NSF-certification visuals
- Wide cinematic establishing shots
- Athlete community montages — high-end captured moments only
- Dual-talent intercuts (DB + Sabalenka, DB + Bearman, etc)
- Mayo Clinic Ventures branding
- Product close-up — label visible, clean surface, considered framing

**Do NOT use:** trend-style fast-cut B-roll, social-media meme overlays, screen recordings, jump cuts, text-heavy graphics, raw phone footage. Those belong to the `ads` mode.

## Brief construction

- Title each brief with a **theme** (e.g. "WORLD CLASS", "THE SCIENCE OF LONGEVITY", "JUST DAVID. JUST THE KITCHEN.") plus a tagline that reads like a brand statement.
- "Why It Works" rationale should explain the brand-building logic — what trust this cut compounds, what audience belief it shifts.
- Avoid hype language in the rationale itself. The brief is internal — sober, strategic.

## Required end frame

```
| [END FRAME] | *[IM8 logo · NSF Certified badge]* |  |
```

## Cut count target

For branded mode, default toward fewer-and-deeper: 5-7 cuts unless source material genuinely supports more.
